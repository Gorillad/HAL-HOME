Color temperature · Kelvin scale

Warm candlelight to cool daylight — pick the atmosphere, then the bulb.

Kelvin (K) describes how warm or cool light looks — not heat, not brightness. Lumens control brightness; Kelvin controls mood. Match Kelvin to the room's job: comfort and flattering glow for living and dining, slightly crisper white for kitchens and baths, and reserve cool tones for task zones — never your chandelier.

2200K Vintage candlelight

Effect: Deep amber glow — intimate, restaurant-style, low visual energy.

Why choose it: Maximum mood on decorative fixtures; pairs with warm-dim LEDs that shift amber as you dim.

Best in your home: Mood dining, bar areas, accent sconces, vintage filament fixtures.

Bulb pick: Amber filament LED · candelabra (E12) · dimmable

2700K Soft warm white

Effect: Classic incandescent warmth — flatters skin, food, wood, brass, and crystal sparkle.

Why choose it: The #1 designer spec for chandeliers and dining — comfortable for hours, never clinical.

Best in your home: Dining room chandelier, living room, primary bedroom, entry statement fixtures.

Bulb pick: Dimmable LED · candelabra (E12) · CRI 90+ · 4–6W per arm

Browse by filter Search · candelabra (E12) · 2700K dimmable
3000K Modern warm white

Effect: Slightly cleaner warm white — sharper edges, still inviting, less yellow than 2700K.

Why choose it: When you want clarity without going cold — good for open plans where kitchen and dining share sightlines.

Best in your home: Kitchen pendants, bath vanity, transitional chandeliers, home office accents.

Bulb pick: 3000K dimmable LED · medium or candelabra base · consistent across the room

Browse by filter Search · 3000K chandelier bulbs
2700K + shape Crystal sparkle

Effect: Maximum refracted fire — visible filament + clear glass makes crystal and cut glass come alive.

Why choose it: Kelvin sets warmth; bulb shape and clear glass set sparkle. Frosted bulbs mute crystal — avoid them here.

Best in your home: Crystal chandeliers, candelabra arms, formal dining, powder room statement pieces.

Bulb pick: Clear filament E12 · 2700K · dimmable · CRI 90+

Browse by filter Search · clear candelabra (E12) filament
4000K – 5000K+ Cool & daylight — skip for chandeliers

Effect: Showroom-clean to blue-white — high alertness, low warmth. Flattened wood, brass, and skin tones.

Why avoid it here: One cool bulb in a warm chandelier reads instantly wrong — the “dentist office” dining room mistake.

Where it belongs: Garage, laundry, workshop, security lighting — task zones, not atmosphere rooms.

Pro rule: Keep every bulb in a fixture — and ideally the whole room — at the same Kelvin.

Read why 2700K wins for dining →

Brightness is lumens, color is Kelvin, accuracy is CRI — a 2700K bulb and a 5000K bulb can match in lumens but feel completely different. For decorative fixtures, spec 2700K · dimmable · CRI 90+ unless the room calls for 3000K.
